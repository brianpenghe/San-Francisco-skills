## Purpose

Summarizes key conclusions from discussions about Weee Rewards

# Weee Rewards

## Weee Points

-   Weee Points obtained by **recharging** are essentially stored-value
    wallet funds.
-   They can be used to pay for future Weee purchases.
-   Recharging does **not** mean the money is spent on membership
    itself.

## Gold Upgrade Promotion

Example promotion: - Normal requirement: Recharge **\$200** Weee
Points. - Promotional requirement: Recharge **\$100** Weee Points. -
Gold membership valid for **3 months**.

### Benefits

-   Local delivery free-shipping threshold reduced:
    -   Silver: **\$49**
    -   Gold: **\$35**
-   Local delivery service fee waived.
-   Member-day products earn **5% rewards**.
-   Better Lightning Deal pricing.
-   Up to 3 Weeebates shares per day.

## Decision Rule

Upgrade is generally worthwhile if: - You expect to spend **\>\$100 on
Weee within the next few months**, since the recharge remains usable as
shopping credit.

Not worthwhile if: - You rarely shop on Weee. - You are unlikely to use
the \$100 balance soon.

# Yogurt Nutrition

## Sugar

Total sugar consists of: 1. Naturally occurring lactose. 2. Added sugar
(if present).

Always check **Added Sugars**, not only Total Sugars.

## Fermentation

Fermentation does **not** eliminate all lactose.

Reasons: 1. Commercial fermentation stops once target acidity is
reached. 2. Many starter cultures hydrolyze lactose but incompletely
metabolize galactose. 3. Typical yogurt retains a substantial amount of
lactose.

## Greek Yogurt

Greek yogurt is strained, removing part of the whey. Since lactose is
mainly dissolved in whey: - Greek yogurt generally contains less sugar
than regular yogurt. - Protein concentration is higher.

## Chobani

### Plain Greek Yogurt

-   Best nutritional choice.
-   No added sugar.
-   High protein.

### Blueberry / Fruit on the Bottom

Approximate per cup: - Protein: \~12 g - Total sugar: \~13--14 g - Added
sugar: \~8--9 g

Suitable if: - Convenience is important.

Healthier alternative: - Plain Greek yogurt + fresh blueberries.

# General Shopping Principle

When evaluating grocery memberships: 1. Determine whether money is
**spent** or merely **prepaid**. 2. Estimate expected spending during
the membership period. 3. Compare actual savings (shipping, service
fees, rewards) against any opportunity cost.
