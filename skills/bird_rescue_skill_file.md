# Skill File: Emergency Rescue & Triage for Wild Birds (Glue Trap & Injury Incidents)

## Overview
This skill file outlines the critical protocol for handling, stabilizing, and securing emergency care for a wild bird (such as an adult sparrow or similar avian wildlife) that has suffered physical damage or exposure to a glue trap.

---

## 1. Immediate Safety & Emergency Assessment
* **Avoid Human Foods & Dairy:** Never feed rescued wild birds cereal, bread, milk, or processed human foods. These can severely harm or kill them (dairy causes fatal digestive distress; dry cereal lacks nutrients and moisture).
* **Treat Glue Traps as High-Priority Emergencies:** Even if a glue trap is labeled "non-toxic", the physical adhesive destroys feather waterproofing/insulation, causes fatal stress, and leads to lethal self-grooming/ingestion of adhesive.
* **Do Not Wash or Pull:** Never use water, soap, solvents (oil, alcohol, dish soap), or manual pulling to remove glue or trapped feathers. Doing so tears paper-thin skin, causes thermal shock, or induces fatal hypothermia.

---

## 2. Overnight Triage & Containment (If No Professional is Immediately Available)
If professional help cannot be reached instantly (e.g., late-night rescue):
1. **Box Containment:** Place the bird inside a secure **cardboard box** lined with a paper towel or soft t-shirt (avoid looped terrycloth towels where claws or damaged feathers snag). Punch small air holes in the lid.
2. **Warmth & Darkness:** * Keep the box **pitch dark and quiet** to prevent panic and capture myopathy (fatal shock).
   * Provide gentle ambient warmth (e.g., a low-setting heating pad under *half* of the box).
3. **No Food or Water:** Do not force-feed, leave standing open water dishes inside the box (risk of drowning), or attempt cleaning overnight.
4. **Leave Stuck to Cardboard:** If the bird is partially attached to a piece of the trap, cut the cardboard around it rather than ripping the bird free.

## 4. Key Action Summary Checklist
- [ ] Place bird in a dark, warm, ventilated cardboard box.
- [ ] Do **not** attempt home baths, solvent cleaning, or forced feeding.
- [ ] Leave attached trap fragments intact on cardboard rather than pulling feathers.
- [ ] Contact local animal control or wildlife dispatch for professional intake.
