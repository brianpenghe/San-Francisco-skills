# California DMV Renewal for an H-1B Holder (REAL ID)

## Purpose

This file combines knowledge about preparing for a California DMV
driver's license renewal appointment as an H-1B visa holder, including
REAL ID, I-797A vs I-797B, and related immigration clarifications.

## DMV Renewal Checklist

### Identity

-   Current California driver's license
-   Passport

### Immigration

-   Printed current I-94
-   Original H-1B approval notice (preferably I-797A if available;
    I-797B is expected for some initial approvals — see below)

### California Residency

Bring any two acceptable proof-of-residency documents. Examples:

-   PG&E bill
-   Water utility bill
-   AT&T bill

Notes:

-   Two documents are sufficient.
-   Consecutive monthly statements are not required.
-   Bank statements are optional if two acceptable utility bills are
    already available.
-   Employment verification is not required when acceptable residency
    documents are provided.

### Appointment

-   Bring the appointment confirmation email or at least the
    confirmation code (recommended but typically not strictly required).

### Vision

-   Bring glasses or contact lenses if required for driving.

## Final Packing List

-   Driver's license
-   Passport
-   Printed I-94
-   Original H-1B approval notice (I-797A or I-797B, as applicable)
-   Two proof-of-residency documents (e.g., PG&E + water bill)
-   Appointment confirmation or confirmation code
-   Glasses/contact lenses (if needed)

## I-94

-   The I-94 is the Arrival/Departure Record documenting current
    immigration status.
-   Printing the current I-94 before the appointment is recommended.
-   The DMV cares about the current I-94, not CBP travel history.
-   Travel history is not required by the DMV.

## I-797A vs I-797B

### I-797A

-   Issued for an extension of stay or change of status inside the U.S.
-   Includes a new I-94.

### I-797B

-   Common for an initial H-1B approved while the beneficiary is outside
    the U.S.
-   Does not include a new I-94.

## Case Summary

-   Initial H-1B approved in 2024.
-   Petition filed while in the UK.
-   Received I-797B.
-   Entered the U.S. using an H-1B visa.
-   CBP issued the current electronic I-94.
-   Therefore, receiving I-797B instead of I-797A is expected.

## Future Extension

If UCSF files an H-1B extension while the beneficiary remains in the
U.S.:

-   USCIS will normally issue I-797A with a new I-94.
-   No international travel is required to obtain I-797A.

## Visa Stamp vs Status

-   Visa stamp is only for entering the U.S.
-   Immigration status is determined by the I-94 and, after extension,
    the I-797A.
-   An expired visa stamp does not by itself mean unlawful presence.

## REAL ID

-   H-1B holders are generally eligible for California REAL ID if they
    can establish lawful presence.
-   A new visa stamp is not required solely to renew a REAL ID after a
    valid extension of stay.
-   Some DMV offices may incorrectly expect an I-797A even when an
    applicant originally entered using an I-797B.
-   Bringing passport, current I-94, and the applicable I-797 notice
    helps verify lawful presence if requested during renewal or REAL ID
    processing.

## Things Not Needed

-   CBP travel history
-   Consecutive utility bills
-   Bank statements (if two other residency documents are available)
-   Employment verification
