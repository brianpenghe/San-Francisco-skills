# Skill: Navigating the San Francisco Community Portal for Homeowners

## Official Portal

**SF.gov/asr-community-portal**

Bookmark this page to access the San Francisco Assessor-Recorder
Community Portal.

## Purpose

Understand when the San Francisco Assessor-Recorder Community Portal is
useful and when it is not.

## Key Takeaways

### 1. The portal is for everyone, not just businesses.

-   Homeowners can create an account.
-   Business owners use separate business-related features.

### 2. Choose the correct section.

-   **Access Real Property** → Residential or commercial real estate
    owners.
-   **Access Business Personal Property** → Businesses reporting taxable
    business equipment and furnishings.

### 3. What the Real Property portal is useful for

-   View property assessment information.
-   Apply for the California Homeowners' Exemption.
-   File eligible property tax exclusions.
-   Update mailing/contact information.
-   Access assessment-related records and services.

### 4. What the portal is *not* primarily for

Property tax payments are handled by the **San Francisco Treasurer & Tax
Collector** payment system, not directly through the Assessor-Recorder
Community Portal.

### 5. Is creating an account worthwhile?

If the only recurring task is paying annual property taxes, the portal
provides limited day-to-day value.

However, it is useful when you need to: - Verify whether a Homeowners'
Exemption has been filed. - Review assessed values. - Submit exemption
or exclusion applications. - Access official property information.

## Practical Recommendation

1.  Create an account once.
2.  Link your property.
3.  Verify your Homeowners' Exemption status.
4.  Pay property taxes through the Treasurer & Tax Collector website.
5.  Return to the Community Portal only when you need property records,
    exemptions, or assessment services.

## Rule of Thumb

If my only task is **paying property tax**, I generally do **not** need
to use the Community Portal regularly. It is primarily a property
records and property management portal rather than a payment portal.
